Singapore sustainability

Regulations, grants, and frameworks

A curated reference for businesses and sustainability professionals navigating Singapore's green transition.

Funding
Grants and financing schemes

Energy Efficiency Grant (EEG)

SME

Up to 70% support for pre-approved energy-efficient equipment in food services, retail, and manufacturing sectors.

Enterprise Singapore

Energy Efficiency Fund (E2F)

Industrial

Supports energy audits, engineering studies, and energy-efficient equipment for industrial facilities.

EMA

Building Retrofit Energy Efficiency Financing (BREEF)

Buildings

Green loan facility at preferential rates for energy-efficient building retrofits and upgrades.

BCA / Financial institutions

Resource Efficiency Grant for Environment (REG(E))

Waste / Water

Supports projects that improve resource efficiency including waste reduction, water use, and energy in the manufacturing sector.

NEA
Certification
Green buildings

BCA Green Mark scheme

Certification

Singapore's green building rating system. Certification levels: Certified, Gold, GoldPLUS, Platinum, and Platinum Super Low Energy (PSLE).

BCA

Green Building MasterPlan

Policy

4th edition masterplan targeting 80% of all buildings by GFA to be Green Mark certified by 2030.

BCA

Super Low Energy (SLE) programme

Performance

Recognition for buildings achieving at least 60% energy improvement over 2005 baselines. Key target for public sector buildings.

BCA

Green Lease Toolkit

Landlord / Tenant

Collaborative framework for landlords and tenants to align on energy management, data sharing, and sustainability targets.

BCA
Disclosure
ESG and sustainability reporting

SGX sustainability reporting

Listed companies

Mandatory for all SGX-listed companies since 2017. Climate-related disclosures aligned with TCFD phased in from FY2022 for large-cap.

SGX RegCo

GRI Standards

Framework

The globally used framework for sustainability reporting. Singapore-listed companies commonly use GRI as the primary reference standard.

GRI

ISSB / IFRS S1 and S2

Mandatory upcoming

MAS has signalled adoption of IFRS S1 (general sustainability disclosures) and S2 (climate disclosures) for Singapore financial institutions.

ISSB / MAS

MAS Environmental Risk Management

Financial sector

Guidelines for banks, insurers, and asset managers on managing environmental risk and integrating climate considerations into governance.

MAS
Policy
Carbon pricing and energy

Singapore Carbon Tax

S$45/tonne in 2026

Applies to facilities emitting 25,000 tCO₂e or more per year. Rising to S$50–80/tonne by 2030 under the Carbon Pricing Act.

NEA

Energy Conservation Act (ECA)

Compliance

Mandatory energy management requirements for corporations consuming over 54 TJ/year. Includes annual reporting and energy efficiency improvement plans.

EMA

Singapore Certified Energy Manager (SCEM)

Certification

Professional certification for energy managers in Singapore. Two levels: SCEM and SCEM (Registered). Required under ECA for qualifying companies.

EMA

Solar energy in Singapore

Clean energy

Singapore's solar target is at least 2 GWp by 2030. SolarNova programme aggregates government demand. Options include rooftop, floating, and building-integrated PV.

EMA / HDB / BCA
Agencies
Key government agencies

Building and Construction Authority (BCA)

Buildings

Governs green building standards, Green Mark certification, and the built environment sustainability agenda.

bca.gov.sg

Energy Market Authority (EMA)

Energy

Regulates the energy sector, administers the Energy Conservation Act, SCEM certification, and energy efficiency grants.

ema.gov.sg

National Environment Agency (NEA)

Environment

Administers carbon tax under the Carbon Pricing Act, waste and resource management, and air quality regulations.

nea.gov.sg

Economic Development Board (EDB)

Industry

Supports industrial decarbonisation, green investments, and sustainability-linked industry development programmes.

edb.gov.sg

Enterprise Singapore

SME

Administers EEG and other SME support schemes. Key entry point for small businesses beginning their sustainability journey.

enterprisesg.gov.sg

Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)

Finance

Drives Singapore's sustainable finance agenda including green taxonomy, ESG disclosure requirements for financial institutions.

mas.gov.sg
